We present \system, a generative and reproducible evaluation framework for multilingual adversarial prompt classification. Existing prompt safety datasets are often English-centric, template-heavy, and evaluated as static artifacts rather than living benchmark grammars. \system reframes the problem around two coupled failure modes: safety bypass, where adversarial prompts pass through to the target model, and availability degradation, where prompts induce refusal spirals, latency inflation, or token burn without a successful compromise. The framework combines a deterministic seven-stage data pipeline, split-before-transform leakage prevention, multilingual and obfuscation robustness suites, and Security--Utility--Efficiency tradeoff analysis. Our central claim is that the grammar of benchmark generation, provenance, and evaluation matters as much as the final released rows. This draft captures the paper boundary around that claim while leaving empirical tables to the evaluation harness as it stabilizes.
